03 — Required Systems (Build Order)

1. Formspree → Redirect to Thank-You Page
Do Today — Free
Right now form submissions go nowhere visible. Add _next: /thank-you to your Formspree form config so every submission lands on the new thank-you page (built this session). This alone recovers your upsell opportunity on every free submission.
Cost: $0 · Time: 5 minutes
2. Email Automation — Loops.so
Free Tier Available
Loops.so is free for up to 1,000 contacts. Connect via Zapier: when a form submission arrives, add contact to Loops → trigger 4-email sequence. Sequence: (1) action plan delivery, (2) demand letter reminder + $29 pitch, (3) filing deadline urgency, (4) final $99 offer. This is your entire sales funnel, automated.
Cost: $0–$49/month depending on list size · loops.so
3. Zapier — The Automation Glue
$19.99/month
Zapier connects Formspree + Square + Loops + Google Sheets + Anthropic API. Without Zapier, each system is isolated. With Zapier, a form submission triggers 5 things simultaneously. The Starter plan ($19.99/mo) covers everything you need at this stage.
Cost: $19.99/month · zapier.com · ROI: One $29 sale covers 2 months
4. Anthropic API — AI Document Generation
~$2–5/month at your volume
This is the heart of autonomous fulfillment. When a free submission arrives, a Zapier webhook calls the Anthropic Claude API with the case description. Claude generates: (1) personalized action plan, (2) demand letter draft, (3) evidence priorities. At ~$0.003 per 1K tokens, processing 100 cases/month costs about $2–5. The prompt should include the user's situation, case type, jurisdiction (Kern County), and instruct Claude to output structured sections.
Cost: ~$2–5/month at current scale · console.anthropic.com
5. Google Sheets — Free CRM
Free
One Google Sheet as your CRM: columns for Name, Email, Phone, Case Type, Amount Owed, Submission Date, Tier Selected, Paid Y/N, Amount Paid, Notes. Zapier auto-populates a new row on every form submission and marks Paid when Square fires a payment event. Gives Jason full pipeline visibility at a glance.
Cost: $0 · google.com/sheets
6. Square → Email Delivery on Payment
Required for $29/$99 autonomy
Right now when someone pays $29 via Square, nothing happens automatically. The fix: Square has a webhook that fires on payment completion → Zapier catches it → triggers a second Claude API call with their case details to generate the full court packet → sends PDF via email. This makes the $29 product fully automated — Jason never touches it.
Cost: $0 additional — Square already in use. Webhook setup in Square Developer Dashboard.
7. PDF Generation — html-pdf-node or Puppeteer via Netlify Function
Optional but professional
For a polished court packet delivery, generate a branded PDF from the Claude output rather than sending plain text email. Options: (A) Use a Netlify serverless function with Puppeteer to render HTML → PDF → email via SendGrid. (B) Use a PDF API like PDFShift ($7/mo) which accepts HTML and returns a PDF. The SmallClaimsPro brand on a proper PDF document increases perceived value significantly.
Cost: $0–$7/month · PDFShift or Netlify Functions
8. Analytics — Google Analytics 4
Free
Add the GA4 tracking snippet to all pages. Track: page views, form submission events, payment link clicks, thank-you page visits (conversion event), and blog traffic. This tells you which Reddit posts and Facebook groups are actually driving conversions vs just traffic. Free and essential.
Cost: $0 · analytics.google.com · Add G-XXXXXXXX to every page head

06 — The AI Prompt for Document Generation

This is the prompt template Zapier sends to the Anthropic API on every free submission. Replace {{variables}} with Zapier-extracted form fields:

You are a California small claims court preparation assistant serving Bakersfield and Kern County residents. A client has submitted the following case: Name: {{name}} Amount Owed: {{amount}} Case Description: {{situation}} Jurisdiction: Kern County Superior Court, Bakersfield CA Generate a structured case analysis with these exact sections: SECTION 1 — CASE ASSESSMENT Brief honest evaluation of case strength (1-2 sentences) SECTION 2 — YOUR ACTION PLAN (3-7 numbered steps) Specific, actionable steps for this exact situation SECTION 3 — DEMAND LETTER DRAFT A complete demand letter in California legal language, ready to customize with their name and the defendant's name. Include: clear statement of debt, deadline (14 days), reference to small claims filing if unpaid. SECTION 4 — EVIDENCE PRIORITIES Top 3-5 pieces of evidence to gather for this specific case type SECTION 5 — KERN COUNTY FILING REMINDER Filing address, fee amount for their claim size, and one sentence about certified mail service Keep the tone clear, direct, and encouraging. This person has been wronged and needs to move forward with confidence. Do not use legal jargon. Write as if explaining to a friend.
