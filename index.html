If you need legal representation, consult a licensed California attorney. For most small claims cases in California, lawyers cannot represent parties at trial anyway — you're on your own in the courtroom regardless.
Individuals can generally claim up to $12,500 in California small claims. Businesses are limited to $6,250. You can file up to two claims per year exceeding $2,500. Always verify the current limit at kern.courts.ca.gov before filing as limits can change.
The Kern County Superior Court handles small claims at 1415 Truxtun Avenue, Bakersfield, CA 93301. Filing hours are Monday–Friday 8am–4pm. Filing fees range from $30 to $75 depending on your claim amount. The court clerk's office can answer procedural questions — they're required to assist you.
California doesn't legally require a demand letter before filing small claims, but judges expect to see that you tried to resolve the dispute first. A certified demand letter strengthens your case significantly and often results in payment before you ever need to step into a courtroom. SmallClaimsPro drafts this letter for you.
